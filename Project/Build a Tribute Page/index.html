Here's a time line of Dr. Stephen Hawking's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England
  • 1952 - Stephen went to St. Albans School and then on to University College, Oxford
  • 1962 - Stephen arrived at the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics (DAMTP) at the University of Cambridge to do research in cosmology
  • 1965 - Completed PhD with his thesis titled 'Properties of Expanding Universes'
  • 1966 -he won the Adams Prize for his essay 'Singularities and the Geometry of Space-time'.
  • 1968- Stephen moved to the Institute of Astronomy
  • 1970 - Hawking postulated what became known as the second law of black hole dynamics, that the event horizon of a black hole can never get smaller
  • 1973 - Moving back to DAMTP, employed as a research assistant, and published his first academic book, The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time, with George Ellis
  • 1974 - Stephen was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society and Sherman Fairchild Distinguished Scholar at the California Institute of Technology
  • 1975 - He became a Reader in Gravitational Physics at DAMTP
  • 1977 - Progressing to Professor of Gravitational Physics
  • 1982 - He was awarded CBE
  • 1989-Companion of Honour
  • 2009 - Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2010- Hawking spoke against the idea that God could have created the universe in his book The Grand Design
  • 2018 -Hawking finally succumbed to the disease that was supposed to have killed him more than 50 years earlier. A family spokesman confirmed that the iconic scientist died at his home in Cambridge, England.
